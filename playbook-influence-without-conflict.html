What not to do 1) Over-explain (it reads as insecurity).
2) Use “I feel” as your main evidence (keep feelings private; use facts in the room).
3) Corner them publicly (status threat = pushback).
4) Ask vague questions (“Any thoughts?”) when you need a decision.
5) Threaten escalation early (keep escalation procedural and late).
Replacement habits (senior moves) 1) Outcome-first opening (“I’m looking for X”).
2) Options + trade-offs (“We can do A or B”).
3) Risk-control language (“Here’s how we contain risk”).
4) Clean close (“Can you confirm yes/no by Thursday?”).
5) Written recap (alignment that sticks).

Chapter 6) Script library (influence lines that work)
These scripts work because they are structured: outcome, options, trade-offs, deadlines, and written alignment. Use them consistently and people stop "testing" your boundaries.

Why it works: it separates intent (“I want the outcome”) from approach (“this route is risky”).

Copy-ready script
I’m aligned on the outcome. I’m not confident this approach gets us there safely. Here’s the risk I’m seeing: <RISK>. Two options that protect the outcome: Option A: <OPTION A> Option B: <OPTION B> Which do you want me to progress?

Positioning: you’re protecting quality and outcomes, not refusing to help.

Copy-ready script
I can take this on, but we’ll need a trade-off to protect delivery. Please choose: A) I prioritise <NEW> and we move/drop <EXISTING>. B) We keep <EXISTING> on track and I start <NEW> on <DATE>. Which do you prefer?

Tip: don’t fight. Name the process and return to the decision.

Copy-ready script
I’m going to pause there so we don’t mix topics. The decision we need is: <DECISION>. I’ll give two options, then we choose: Option A: <A> Option B: <B> Which do you want me to proceed with?

Principle: acknowledge risk, then show containment.

Copy-ready script
You’re right to focus on risk. Here’s what we’re controlling: - Risk: <RISK> - Control: <CONTROL> - Checkpoint: <WHEN WE REVIEW> If any of the red flags appear, we stop and revert. With that in place, can you confirm we proceed with <OPTION>?
Copy-ready script
Quick recap of what we agreed: 1) Outcome: <OUTCOME> 2) Decision: we’re proceeding with <OPTION> 3) Trade-offs: we are stopping/deferring <TRADE-OFF> 4) Owner + deadline: <OWNER> by <DATE> If anything above is incorrect, please reply today so I can adjust.
Personality / Behaviour lens Dominant: keep it short. Analytical: document logic. Agreeable: warm tone, firm boundary. Avoidant: yes/no by a date.
Micro-skill: pause before you respond A 2-second pause reads as control. Fast responses read as reactivity. Influence likes patience.
