EVIDENCE & DOCUMENTATION TEMPLATE

Build a Strong Clear Record That Protects You and Supports a Calm Confident Escalation

A structured, copy-ready documentation system for workplace issues: what happened, when it happened, what you can evidence, and how to write it so it stays factual, professional, and hard to dismiss.
This tool helps you document clearly and safely. It isn’t legal advice. If you feel unsafe or there’s immediate risk, prioritise safety and urgent support first.

When to Use This Template

  • Before raising something with HR, ER, a wellbeing lead, or your manager’s manager.
  • When you suspect you’re being managed out (shifting expectations, moving goalposts, pressure tactics).
  • When you need to request adjustments, protect your performance standing, or rebut inaccurate claims.
  • After an incident, difficult meeting, or message exchange that may later be reframed.
  • When you want a calm, professional “paper trail” without sounding emotional or accusatory.

Pro Tips

  • Write like a professional witness: who/what/when/whereand avoid motives and labels.
  • Keep each entry time-bounded: one event, one impact, one evidence reference.
  • Use exact quotes sparingly and accurately, only where it matters.
  • Assume everything could be forwarded. Every line should stand up to daylight.
  • Evidence is not “what you feel”. Evidence is what you can show: messages, outputs, dates, decisions, patterns.

The witness standard

Use this structure: “On [date], in [context], [person] said/did [behaviour/quote]. Impact: [effect].”

Separate “facts” from “interpretation”

Facts go in the record. Interpretation goes in your private notes — only include interpretation if it’s necessary and you can tie it to evidence.

Quick Coverage Checklist

This is your audit before you hit send. It catches the mistakes that cause dismissal, exposure, or “he said / she said” outcomes.
I’ve defined the issue clearly (type, window, neutral case label).
My facts are time-bounded and specific (who/what/when/where), not motives or labels.
I have a chronology that links each event to impact and evidence reference.
I can point to at least 3–5 concrete proof points (messages/notes/outputs).
I know what I’m missing and how I will safely capture it (without policy risk).
My message is procedural and outcome-led (process, clarity, next steps).
My requested outcome is specific, reasonable, and written down.
I’ve captured who was involved and the channels used (useful for investigations).
