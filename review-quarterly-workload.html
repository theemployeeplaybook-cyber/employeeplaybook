When to Use This

  • End of quarter (or after a restructure / scope creep wave).
  • When you feel “always busy” but delivery is slipping.
  • When your role has quietly expanded (more stakeholders, more admin, more meetings).
  • Before performance reviews — so you can show trade-offs, not excuses.
  • Before you agree to new commitments or stretch goals.

Tips & Tricks (premium)

  • Separate “work” from “workload”. Workload includes meetings, context-switching, admin, stakeholder churn.
  • Quantify with ranges. “3–5 hrs/week of X” is harder to dismiss than “it’s a lot.”
  • Use neutral language. “Capacity risk” beats “I’m overwhelmed” in most cultures.
  • Offer options. “If we keep A, we drop B or reduce C.” Make the decision easy.
  • Protect deep work. One uninterrupted 90–120 min block/day is a delivery multiplier.
Quick win: If your week has > 12 hours of meetings, aim to convert 25–40% into async updates.
