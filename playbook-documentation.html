Chapter 7) Safety, privacy & boundaries
Documentation should protect you — not create new risk. This section keeps your approach clean: privacy, policy, and self-protection.
Write like a witnessDate/time, context, exact words, impact, follow-up. Avoid labels. Avoid diagnoses.
Keep artefacts tidyOne place. Clear names. Easy retrieval. If you can’t find it fast, it can’t protect you.
Use “alignment” language“To confirm”, “to clarify”, “to avoid drift”, “for accuracy”. It stays non-accusatory.
Regulate firstIf your body is activated, your writing will leak emotion. Pause, then recap.
Your personal rule set
If there’s harassment, discrimination, or threatsDon’t “wait and see”. Document fast, seek support, and use formal routes early.
Protective moveBring a witness to key meetings, request agendas, and confirm outcomes in writing immediately.
