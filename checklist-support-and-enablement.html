SUPPORT & ENABLEMENT CHECKLIST

Are you genuinely supported to succeed

When expectations are high but support feels thin, this checklist helps you spot the gaps like access, context, cover, decision-rights and stakeholder backing before they quietly become performance risk.
Explore Categories
HR Insider Confidence, Presence & Influence

When to Use This Checklist

  • When you feel you are being judged on outcomes without proper support
  • When you’ve joined a new team, inherited a mess, or scope has shifted
  • When “just get it done” is replacing clarity, enablement and cover
  • When mistakes are treated as personal failure rather than a support gap
  • Before accepting accountability for delivery risks you can’t control

Pro Tips

  • Support gaps are often invisible until something goes wrong
  • It’s safer to raise enablement early than explain it later
  • Enablement is not “help” — it’s access, context, decision-rights and backing
  • Keep your tone factual: “what’s needed to deliver” not “what’s unfair”
  • Document gaps quietly even if you don’t escalate yet

Your Checklist

I am clear on what I own, what I don’t own, and where accountability sits.
I have the tools, access, systems and permissions needed to deliver properly.
I have enough background and context to make good decisions, not guess.
My manager actively removes blockers rather than only applying pressure.
I know which decisions I can make and which require approval or cover.
I receive timely, specific feedback early enough to adjust course.
I have guidance or training for new responsibilities (not just expectations).
Key stakeholders support the work rather than bypassing me or shifting blame.
Risks are shared openly, not quietly transferred to me.
I am backed publicly when things are uncertain, not left exposed.
Enablement is proactive (set up to succeed), not only offered after problems.
The performance bar matches the level of support and control I genuinely have.
