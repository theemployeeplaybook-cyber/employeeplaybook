When to Use This Builder

  • When you’re doing “next level” work but it isn’t being seen or credited.
  • Before performance reviews, calibration, or promotion panels.
  • When you need to ask for scope, title, or pay alignment.
  • When you’ve been told “not yet” and need a clearer path to “yes”.
  • When you want to lead the narrative instead of waiting to be “noticed”.

What This Builder Does

  • Helps translate achievements into business impact stories.
  • Structures your case around organisational priorities.
  • Provides language for discussing growth without sounding entitled.
  • Creates a “promotion-ready” summary plus scripts for the conversation.
  • Produces an output you can save, print, and refine over time.

Credibility rule: Don’t compare yourself to colleagues. Compare your output to the expectations of the next level — and anchor to business outcomes.

Momentum & positioning

Alignment rule: Every achievement should map to at least one organisational priority — that’s how you make it “hard to say no”.

Career psychology lens
This reduces “vague” rejection (“not quite ready”) by tightening your signals: trust, risk, status, and who is willing to sponsor you. It also helps you speak to how decisions really get made — not just effort.

Signal rule: Promotions often follow a simple question: “Would I trust this person with bigger, messier problems — without supervision?” Your job is to make the answer feel obvious.
