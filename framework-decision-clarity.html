How to use this framework

Decision clarity is not certainty. It's getting to a choice you can explain, commit to, and execute — without reopening it every day.

Use this when you're stuck between options, waiting for "more information", or looping in over-analysis. You'll make the options explicit, surface the real trade-offs, stress-test risk, and finish with a commitment statement + next action.

If a decision cannot survive honest trade-offs, it isn't a decision — it's a wish.

Decision friction patterns

Most “hard decisions” are not a lack of intelligence — they’re a clash of incentives. Use this as a mirror: name the pattern, then build the decision around it.

Perfection loop
You keep refining because choosing means closing doors. Fix: define “good enough” and set a deadline.
Conflict avoidance
You delay because the decision creates a difficult conversation. Fix: write the conversation first.
Info addiction
You’re waiting for “one more data point”. Fix: decide what evidence would actually change your mind.
Identity fear
You’re protecting an image of yourself. Fix: choose the option that protects long-term integrity, not short-term comfort.
1
Define the decision Strip it down to what is actually being decided — and by when.
0/4 complete
What is the decision? (one sentence)
What is NOT being decided here?
What happens if you do nothing?
When does this decision realistically need to be made?
Rule: if you cannot state the decision clearly, you are not ready to decide. Clarity starts with a clean problem statement.
2
Options on the table Make the real options explicit — including the uncomfortable one.
0/4 complete
Option A (plain description)
Option B (plain description)
Option C (walk away / delay / redesign)
Which option are you already leaning toward?
Reality check: avoid fake options you would never choose. They inflate fear and reduce honesty.
3
Trade-offs & costs Replace vague fear with concrete consequences.
0/4 complete
What do you gain with your preferred option?
What do you lose with your preferred option?
What will it cost you in energy / reputation / time?
What problem does your preferred option create?
Lens: clarity comes from choosing which problem you are willing to live with. Trade-offs are the truth of the decision.
4
Risk & commitment Stress-test reversibility, then lock the choice with a commitment statement.
0/4 complete
What is the worst realistic outcome?
Is this reversible — and what is your recovery plan?
Commitment statement (fill the sentence)
First concrete action (and when)
Reframe: irreversible decisions require caution. Reversible decisions require action. Either way, finish with a commitment you can execute.
