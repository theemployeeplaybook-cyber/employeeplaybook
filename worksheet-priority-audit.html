PRIORITY AUDIT WORKSHEET

Stop “everything is urgent” and rebuild a priority system that survives real life

This worksheet forces clarity under pressure: what you’re doing, what it costs, what’s actually due, and what deserves focus. It gives you an evidence-led way to reset expectations with your manager without sounding dramatic — just organised.

Use it when your day is being eaten by reactive work, unclear requests, or “quiet scope creep” that never gets formally agreed.

When to Use This Worksheet

  • When you’re doing too much, but can’t explain it cleanly.
  • When priorities change daily and “trade-offs” never get agreed.
  • When your workload is being shaped by the loudest request, not the highest value.
  • When you need a calm reset conversation with your manager, grounded in reality.
  • When you need a list that’s credible enough to survive scrutiny.

How It Works

  • Capture your current work and score it with a simple model: urgency + impact, adjusted by effort and risk.
  • Sort by score to reveal what is actually driving outcomes vs what is just noise.
  • Identify 3 focus moves: what to do, what to defer, what to delegate or stop.
  • Generate a Priority Audit Pack you can paste into an email or use in a reset meeting.
  • Keep it tight: the goal is a decision, not a debate.
