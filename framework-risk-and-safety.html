RISK & SAFETY FRAMEWORK

Assess risk without panic and take action without exposure

A premium, practical framework for workplace risk: triage, severity, evidence, reporting routes, and a calm protection plan you can execute in the next 72 hours.
This tool supports structured thinking and documentation. It isn’t legal advice. If there’s immediate danger or you feel unsafe right now, prioritise getting to safety and contacting emergency/security support.
Related Categories
Expectations, Burnout & Capacity Workplace Dynamics & Politics

When to Use This Framework

  • When something feels “off” and you need to separate risk from emotion, gossip, or assumptions.
  • When behaviour is escalating (pressure, intimidation, retaliation risk) and you need a safe plan.
  • Before you report a concern — so your report is tight, factual, and hard to dismiss.
  • When you’re unsure whether to handle quietly, document, or escalate.
  • When you need to protect your wellbeing, role security, and credibility at the same time.

Pro Tips

  • Risk is not “how bad it feels”. Risk is impact × likelihood, plus exposure if mishandled.
  • Write like a professional witness: who/what/when/where, not motives.
  • Assume your message may be forwarded. Every line should stand up to daylight.
  • Always have a “safe alternative”: a reversible next step that gathers evidence and reduces exposure.
  • If you’re under pressure to keep it informal, that’s often a signal you should document.

Triage principle

When safety or retaliation risk exists, your first goal is exposure reduction: fewer private interactions, more written clarity, and a clean record.

The witness sentence

Use this structure: “On [date], in [context], [person] said/did [quote/behaviour]. Impact: [effect].”

What works reliably

  • Move from private to professional: written recaps, agenda-led meetings, fewer ambiguous side chats.
  • Neutral language: “risk / impact / process” beats “toxic / unfair / malicious”.
  • Reversibility first: if you’re unsure, choose a move that reduces exposure and gathers evidence.

Process integrity lens

When risk is non-trivial, you’re protecting the process as much as yourself: clarity on who owns the case, what happens next, and how information is handled.

Optics control

What not to do

  • Don’t litigate motives: it invites argument. Stick to behaviour + impact + process.

Quick Coverage Checklist

The canvas above is the framework. This checklist is your final audit: it catches the common failure points that cause exposure, dismissal, or messy escalation.
I’ve triaged immediate danger and prioritised safety/exposure reduction before “explaining” anything.
My description is factual (who/what/when/where) and avoids motives, labels, or diagnoses.
I’ve preserved evidence (messages, notes, dates) and started a timeline.
I’ve rated severity (impact × likelihood) and identified exposure multipliers (retaliation, isolation, pattern).
I know the safest reporting route (especially if the line manager is involved).
I’ve mapped who can materially help (HR/ER, H&S, senior, rep) and what each optimises for.
I have a short protection plan (72 hours) that reduces exposure and locks the record.
My language is procedural and outcome-led (risk/process/safety), not personal or accusatory.
