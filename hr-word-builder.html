When to Use This Builder

  • When you need to raise an issue without triggering defensiveness or “tone policing”.
  • When you want HR to take you seriously: facts, timeline, impact, and a clean ask.
  • When you’re sending a follow-up after a tense meeting and need language that holds.
  • When you need to request adjustments, support, a case owner, or clarity on process.
  • When your emotions are valid — but your wording needs to be strategically neutral.
HR responds faster to clarity than intensity.

How to Use It (fast)

  • Write like a neutral reviewer will read it: dates, outcomes, impact, ask.
  • Keep claims observable. Swap “unfair” for “criteria not communicated”.
  • Ask for process and decisions, not approval or sympathy.
  • Use one sentence to signal risk (delivery / health / conduct) then return to facts.
  • Always end with: what you need + by when + next step.
