COMMUNICATION PLANNING TEMPLATE

Plan what you’ll say, who it’s for, and what success looks like so your message is clear and drives decisions

Move from emotion to architecture: objective, audience, message, proof, tone, channel, timing, and follow-up. Reduce misinterpretation, protect credibility, and make your communication harder to ignore, without being aggressive.

When to Use This Template

  • Before a difficult 1:1, pushback, boundary reset, or escalation email.
  • When you need a decision and want to avoid “we’ll circle back”.
  • When you’re worried your message could be misread, politicised, or minimised.
  • When you need to communicate change, risk, or impact without sounding dramatic.
  • When you want a calm paper trail that holds under scrutiny.

How to Use It (fast)

  • Define one objective and one measurable success outcome.
  • Write your key message in a single sentence that survives forwarding.
  • Include proof points: facts, impact, dates, examples.
  • Choose tone deliberately (not based on mood) and match it to stakes.
  • Lock the follow-up: owner, timeline, review date, “if not resolved then…”

Draft your communication plan

Four structured sections: (1) objective + stakes, (2) audience + decision context, (3) message + proof + tone, (4) channel + timing + follow-up. Generate your Comms Pack when you have enough to be specific and action-led.
