Overview

What this category covers

Use this category when you feel your voice isn't being heard, you're being overlooked for opportunities, or you want to increase your influence without formal authority. It's built for building the presence and communication skills needed to advance your career and make greater impact.

When to use this category

  • You struggle to speak up in meetings or share your ideas confidently.
  • You feel overlooked for promotions or high-visibility projects.
  • You want to build influence without formal authority or title.
  • You need to communicate complex ideas more effectively.
  • You want to project more confidence and authority in your role.

If the main issue is about your relationship with your manager, try Manager Relationship & Leadership Style instead. For workplace politics involving multiple stakeholders, try Workplace Dynamics & Politics.

What this category helps you do:

  • Speak up confidently in meetings and presentations.
  • Communicate complex ideas with clarity and impact.
  • Build influence and credibility without formal authority.
  • Handle challenging conversations with poise.
  • Project professional presence that gets you noticed for the right reasons.
Signals

How you'll recognise this pattern

You'll recognise it if:

You feel your contributions are overlooked or undervalued in team settings.

  • You hesitate to speak up in meetings, even when you have valuable input.
  • Others with similar experience seem to progress faster or get more visibility.
  • You feel nervous before presentations or important conversations.
  • Your ideas are often credited to others or dismissed without consideration.
  • You avoid taking credit for your work or downplay your achievements.

Typical pain points:

A lack of confidence and presence can limit your career trajectory and impact.

  • Missing out on promotions, raises, or high-visibility projects.
  • Feeling frustrated when less experienced colleagues advance faster.
  • Struggling to get buy-in for your ideas and initiatives.
  • Feeling invisible or undervalued despite strong performance.
  • Anxiety about speaking up or taking on leadership opportunities.
Patterns

Common confidence patterns you might experience

Understanding these patterns helps you identify where you might be getting stuck and which strategies will be most effective for building your presence and influence.

Pattern 1
The Imposter Syndrome Cycle
  • Doubt your abilities despite evidence of competence.
  • Attribute success to luck or external factors.
  • Fear being "found out" as not qualified.
  • Overprepare or avoid opportunities to prevent exposure.
Strategy: Collect evidence of your competence. Track accomplishments and positive feedback.
Pattern 2
The Quiet Voice
  • Struggle to speak up in group settings.
  • Process ideas internally while discussions move on.
  • Have valuable insights but share them too late or not at all.
  • Feel overshadowed by more vocal colleagues.
Strategy: Prepare talking points in advance. Practice concise contributions before meetings.
Pattern 3
The Undervalued Contributor
  • Consistently deliver strong work but receive little recognition.
  • Focus on execution over visibility and communication.
  • Assume good work will speak for itself.
  • Watch others get credit for similar or lesser contributions.
Strategy: Systematically document and communicate your achievements and impact.
Application

Your core moves in this category

These moves define your strategic approach to building confidence, presence, and influence. Use them to shape your interactions, increase your visibility, and communicate with greater impact.

Move 1
Prepare to perform
  • Structure your thoughts before important meetings or presentations.
  • Practice key messages and anticipate questions.
  • Create visual or written prompts to stay on track.
  • Focus on your value-add rather than perfection.
Preparation reduces anxiety and increases your ability to contribute effectively in real-time.
Move 2
Communicate with clarity and impact
  • Structure your communication for different audiences.
  • Lead with key messages rather than background details.
  • Use storytelling to make ideas memorable and persuasive.
  • Practice concise, confident delivery.
Clear communication demonstrates competence and makes your contributions more valuable.
Move 3
Build strategic visibility
  • Identify key stakeholders and decision-makers in your organization.
  • Seek opportunities to contribute in high-visibility forums.
  • Document and share your achievements strategically.
  • Build relationships that increase your influence network.
Visibility creates opportunities. Strategic visibility ensures you're noticed for the right reasons.
