When things hit crisis point

Fast stabilisation: protect yourself, reduce escalation, keep options open

Toxic workplaces don’t just make you feel bad — they can seriously impact your wellbeing, confidence, career trajectory, and life outside work. If something feels wrong at work, it often is. And you don’t have to carry it alone.

One of the first protective moves
  • Keep notes as soon as you can after an incident. It helps you stay grounded and gives you a clear record if you later need it.
  • Include the date you wrote the note and the date the event happened.
  • Write who was there and where you were.
  • Capture your best memory of what happened and what was said (keep it factual and specific).
When behaviour may cross into a legal issue
  • If it relates to discrimination (protected characteristics).
  • If it looks like a reaction to you using a protected right (for example, raising concerns or asserting workplace rights).
  • If it suggests a fundamental breach of contract (serious employer conduct that undermines trust and confidence).

Your feelings are valid. This section is here to help you take steady, protective steps and choose what to do next. If you need formal legal advice, you should speak to a qualified employment solicitor.
