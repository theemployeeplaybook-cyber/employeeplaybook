PRIORITY RESET PLAN

Stop the chaos, pick the real priorities, and reset your week with authority

Use the workspace to surface what’s truly on your plate, identify what actually matters, and make clean decisions: drop, defer, delegate, or deliver. Then generate a Priority Reset Pack: a one-page priority map, quiet-risk prompts (to prevent drift), and scripts to align others without sounding defensive.

When to Use This Plan

  • When everything feels urgent and you’re reacting instead of leading.
  • When you’ve inherited work, vague asks, or “drive-bys” that keep multiplying.
  • When your calendar is full but impact is low.
  • When you’re carrying other people’s priorities because nobody is deciding.
  • When you need a clean reset without drama, excuses, or over-explaining.

How to Use It (fast)

  • Write bluntly. This is for clarity, not aesthetics.
  • Separate real commitments from noise (opinions, anxiety, “maybe” work).
  • Pick a small number of priorities that you can actually deliver — then protect them.
  • Use the scripts to align stakeholders early, before drift becomes blame.
  • Click Generate Priority Reset Pack when you’ve made real decisions (drop/defer/delegate).
