When to Use This Plan

  • When “just this week” has become your permanent operating system.
  • When you are delivering, but it’s powered by late nights, adrenaline, and constant catch-up.
  • When you feel wired after work and your weekends are recovery, not life.
  • When the job keeps expanding because you keep absorbing the gaps.
  • When you need to protect both output and reputation — not collapse in private and perform in public.

How to Use It (fast)

  • Start with reality: map your week as it truly runs, not how it “should” run.
  • Identify your pace archetype — it will explain why you keep drifting into overwork.
  • Choose 3 guardrails that change expectations (not just your coping).
  • Use Pressure Mode when you’re running hot: it gives you a 48-hour triage route that protects sleep and credibility.
  • A sustainable pace is a design problem (priorities, capacity, boundaries) as much as a personal one.

Pressure Mode: 48-hour triage (without a reputation dent)

Use this when you’re running hot: overload, exhaustion, constant urgency, or you need sustainable pace and recovery built into delivery. This overlay helps you stop the slide, reduce load fast, and convert urgency into clear trade-offs.

48-hour triage steps

  • 1
    Stop the leak
    Pause new intake for 24 hours. Reply with a holding line and require priorities/trade-offs before accepting more.
  • 2
    Convert urgency into a decision
    Ask: “What moves if this comes in?” If nobody will name the trade-off, it is not a real priority.
  • 3
    Protect sleep as a delivery dependency
    Choose one non-negotiable: hard stop time, no late emails, or a short reset block. Your output depends on it.
  • 4
    Make the plan visible
    Send a short note with priorities + what you’re not doing. Clarity reduces “surprise urgency”.
