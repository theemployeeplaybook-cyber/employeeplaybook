Chapter 2) Frame + boundaries
Boundaries are not speeches. They're short sentences plus consistent follow-through. Your aim is to be calm, clear, and predictably hard to push around.
Rule: one line, no apology, no over-explaining. If you explain too much, you invite negotiation.

Chapter 4) Scripts + tactics
Difficult people often win by forcing you into the wrong mode: defending, pleading, over-explaining, or exploding. These scripts keep you in clarity mode: decisions, boundaries, and written proof.
Threat reduction languageUse “alignment” words: clarify, agree, decide, sequence. It keeps you firm without sounding combative.
Less-known tactic: “pause the game”When they bait you, don’t chase. Pause: “Let’s come back to the decision.” Repeat once. Then move to writing.

Goal: regain structure without confrontation. Your tool is process: agenda, turn-taking, decision capture.

Can we pause for 10 seconds and anchor on the decision we're making? I'm going to summarise in one line, then we can choose between two options. Option A: <OPTION A> (impact: <IMPACT>). Option B: <OPTION B> (impact: <IMPACT>). Which are we choosing — and who owns next steps?

Goal: remove the "game" and return to facts. Do not mirror the tone.

I'm going to stay on the work. If there's a concern, can you name it directly — and what you want to be different? Otherwise, let's stick to the decision and next steps.

Goal: correct the record calmly. Make it about accuracy, not ego.

Just to clarify the record for accuracy: I led <WORK> and delivered <OUTCOME>. Happy to share the artefacts/notes if useful. Next step is <NEXT STEP> by <DATE>.

Goal: force trade-offs. Scope changes are fine — pretending they are "free" is not.

We can add <NEW ITEM>, but it changes the plan. To keep this clean, which do you want to trade off: A) Move the deadline to <NEW DATE> or B) Drop <ITEM TO DROP>? If neither works, we need a different owner/resourcing decision.

Goal: anchor to artefacts, not debate. Notes > opinions.

To keep us aligned, here's what I captured in the recap on <DATE>: • Decision: <DECISION> • Owner: <OWNER> • Deadline: <DEADLINE> If anything is incorrect, please reply with amendments — otherwise I'll proceed on that basis.

Goal: refuse the triangle. Bring it into the open, or end the conversation.

I don't want to work through third-hand messages. If there's a concern, let's bring the person into the conversation or capture it in writing with clear specifics. Otherwise, I'm not going to speculate.

Goal: make silence costly (professionally). Use deadlines and default actions.

To keep delivery moving, I need a decision on <DECISION> by <DATE/TIME>. If I don't hear back, I'll proceed with the default option <DEFAULT> and note it in the delivery update. If you prefer a different route, reply with the option and any constraints.
