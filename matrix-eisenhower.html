Sort urgency from importance

When to Use the Matrix

  • You’re overwhelmed and everything feels “urgent”, but your important work is stalling.
  • You’re being pulled into constant requests, Slack pings, or last-minute changes.
  • You need a calm way to say “not now”, “not me”, or “not necessary” without drama.
  • You’re preparing for a 1:1 and want clarity on what you’re doing vs what you’re absorbing.
  • You need a visible rationale for re-prioritising (especially in political or high-pressure environments).

Pro Tips (Use These Rules)

  • Urgent is about time pressure. Important is about impact, risk, and outcomes.
  • If Q1 is full, your system is broken: you’re either under-resourced, unclear, or absorbing other people’s panic.
  • Q2 is where your future stress is prevented. Protect it like a meeting with your reputation.
  • Q3 is a boundary tool. If it doesn’t require your unique skill, route it away from your focus.
  • Q4 is not “later”. It’s “no”. Delete, decline, minimise, or stop feeding it attention.
