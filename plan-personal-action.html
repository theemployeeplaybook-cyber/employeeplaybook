When to Use This

  • When you need a plan that can survive real workloads and workplace politics.
  • When you feel stuck: overthinking, delay, or “I’ll do it next week”.
  • When you need to make a decision and then follow through without wobbling.
  • When you need clarity, evidence, and clean scripts — not emotional improvisation.
  • When you need a weekly rhythm for execution and review (not a one-off burst).

How to Use It (fast)

  • Select your reason first. The prompts and scripts will re-tune automatically.
  • Define a measurable target (not a vibe like “better” or “less stressed”).
  • Choose actions that change reality — not endless prep.
  • Lock decisions in writing. That’s how you beat drift and shifting goalposts.
  • Review weekly. Keep the plan alive without turning it into a drama series.
