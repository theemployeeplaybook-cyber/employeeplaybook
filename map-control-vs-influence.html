Control vs Influence: smart moves
Practical notes
  • Don’t confuse confidence with control: the loudest person often has influence, not authority.
  • Find the approval path: who signs, who reviews, who can pause—then map your “yes sequence”.
  • Pre-alignment beats persuasion in-room: influence works best before the meeting, not during it.
  • Use “constraint language” with control: “To approve this safely, we need…” makes decisions easier.
